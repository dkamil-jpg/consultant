Kamil Dyczkowski
Process & Systems Expert

I am not a motivational speaker and not a typical consultant. I have spent 20+ years on the operational frontline – in manufacturing, warehouses, and service environments – learning (often the hard way) how to close the gap between how work is described and how it actually happens.

I step in when process integrity has hit its limit and the business is being capped by its own chaos. My scope covers manufacturing, warehousing, and appointment-driven service operations – from treatment studios and clinics to high-volume trade and workshop services.

The goal is simple: stable, calm operations. Fewer exceptions, clearer priorities, and one reliable source of truth your team can depend on under pressure. Best practice is used to create order and predictability – not bureaucracy.

My work follows a straight line:

  • Understand how work really happens today (not how it is supposed to happen)
  • Redesign the workflow so it survives growth, variability, and imperfect days
  • If needed, implement an enforcement layer so incorrect actions are prevented or exposed immediately

Enforcement is not about controlling people. It is about controlling the process – so teams cannot accidentally create errors, and the business does not rely on memory, heroics, or constant checking.

Over two decades in high-stakes environments, I have seen where even large systems fail – not because they are weak, but because they are built on assumptions that do not match the reality of the shop floor or the reception desk. I design operations and systems without those assumptions, around what actually happens.

My background is Lean and continuous improvement, but the approach is aggressive practicality. No certificates, no theatre – only what holds under real conditions: tired people, interruptions, edge cases, and incomplete information.

I have a specific talent for spotting inefficiencies and failure modes most owners and mainstream consultants overlook. Where others see "just how we do things", I see a leak that burns time, eats profit, damages trust, and frustrates the team.

My way of working is practical, evidence-driven, and shaped by deep study of neurobiology and behavioural science – because operational reliability is ultimately human reliability, engineered into the process.
