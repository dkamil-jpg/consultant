Kamil Dyczkowski
Process & Systems Expert

I am not a motivational speaker and not a typical consultant. I am someone who has spent over 20 years on the operational frontline – in manufacturing, warehouses and service environments – learning, often through mistakes, how to close the gap between how work is described and how it actually happens.

My scope covers manufacturing, warehouses and appointment-driven service operations, from treatment studios and clinics to high-volume trade and workshop services. I enter when process integrity has reached its limit and the business is capped by its own chaos.

The goal is simple: stable, calm operations. Fewer exceptions, clearer priorities, and one reliable source of truth that your team can depend on under pressure. Best practices are used to create order and predictability, not bureaucracy.

My work follows a straight line:

  • understand how work really happens today,
  • design a process that can survive growth,
  • and, if needed, implement a strict enforcement layer in software so incorrect actions are either prevented or exposed immediately.

Enforcement is not about controlling people. It is about controlling the process, so teams cannot accidentally create errors and nobody has to rely on memory to keep the business running.

Over more than 20 years in high-stakes environments, I have seen where even large systems fail – not because they are weak, but because they are built on assumptions that do not match the reality of the shop floor or the reception desk. I design and build systems without those assumptions, around what actually happens in your operation.

My background is in Lean and continuous improvement, but my approach is aggressive practicality. I am not interested in certificates or theatre. I am interested in whether your operation can handle tomorrow without falling apart.

I have a specific talent for spotting inefficiencies in places most business owners and popular market consultants overlook. Where others see "just how we do things", I see a leak that damages your reputation, burns time, eats into profit, and generates frustration.

I do not compromise on process integrity. If we build it, it must work under real conditions – with tired people, interruptions and imperfect information.

My way of working is not theoretical. It is the result of over 20 years on the operational frontline, supported by deep study of neurobiology and behavioural science.
