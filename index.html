Is this your situation?

Diagnosis

You have a process problem

You've been running your business for years with the same loyal people. They know the job, but the way work is done hasn't really changed since the 1990s.

Orders get printed. Someone walks the warehouse with a piece of paper and a pen, ticking what they managed to pick and circling what they couldn't find. Every extra customer means more chaos, more "emergency plans", more overtime – and a growing risk of sending the wrong item or missing something entirely.

Your real problem is not effort or loyalty. It's that the process no longer matches the volume and complexity of your business.

Diagnosis

You have a system problem

You already know roughly what you want:

  • a self check-in kiosk and a client database for a clinic or salon,
  • a front-desk system for a workshop,
  • or a simple tool to track visits, jobs or bookings.

You don't want another generic SaaS that almost fits and forces you to bend your process around it. You want a small, focused system that matches how you actually want to work – without paying enterprise-grade fees or waiting a year for delivery.

In both cases, the pattern is the same: the way you work and the tools you use are out of sync. That's where I come in.

How I work

1

I watch how work really happens

I don't start with a workshop or a slide deck. I spend up to five days on site, on your floor – following orders, stock, people and decisions through your actual day. I talk to the people who really do the work, not just the ones who write procedures.

2

I map the leaks and failure points

Together we make visible where time, money and trust are leaking: searching, rework, wrong picks, missing information, fragile handovers, "only John knows how this is done". You get a clear picture of what is actually happening, not what's written in a manual.

3

I design a process that can survive growth

I redesign the process so that correct actions become the default and mistakes become hard to make. Rules are clear, handovers are explicit and you can scale work without doubling the chaos and overtime. If a better process alone is enough, we stop here.

4

If needed, I build the system around that process

Only if the process needs support from software do we talk about systems. I design and deliver a small, focused application that fits your operation: warehouse tools for pickers, simple reception/check-in systems, or internal tools for tracking work. The process comes first. The system follows.

What you get

  • A brutally honest picture of how your operation really runs today.
  • A process that your team can actually follow under pressure – not just when everyone is fresh and everything goes to plan.
  • Fewer mistakes in picking, dispatch, booking or front-desk work – because the process makes errors hard, not because you told people to "be more careful".
  • The option to have a simple, custom system built around your process, instead of bending your work around a generic tool.
  • A way to measure whether things are getting better or worse – not just "it feels calmer" or "it feels worse".

Pricing & guarantee

Most on-site diagnostics and process redesign projects for a single operation (for example, one warehouse or one front-of-house) start from £1,500–£3,000, depending on size and complexity. This covers up to five days on site in your business and the design work needed to stabilise one core process.

Custom software and systems are quoted separately once we know how simple or complex your process needs to be. There is no obligation to commission a system – in many cases, a better process is enough.

Simple guarantee on the diagnostic phase

Within those five days on site, if I cannot:

  • identify a concrete operational problem I can help you fix, or
  • design a realistic process and/or system for your situation, or
  • show a projected benefit that is clearly higher than my fee,

I will not take the project forward – and I will refund my fee for this diagnostic and design phase.

This guarantee covers my side of the table: if I misjudge the fit, you don't carry the risk. What I cannot guarantee is what your team does with a working process and system once I leave.
